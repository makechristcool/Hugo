Unapologetically Kind

They say a raccoon lives between two worlds. I never thought that description would be aimed at me — by someone from my own community. Wait, that started way to hot and I didn’t even introduce myself. I’m Lindsey, and I don’t believe Blacks need to “Black” everything. I think our ancestors dealt with a lot more than we (modern day black people) will ever deal with. I told my colleague we have a lot more advantages than our predecessors and we have tools to counter racism or what we call prejudice. I was able to attend college without having the best GPA, and I work alongside people with double master’s degrees. Somehow I’m black and ended up in the same place as my white counterparts. My colleague didn’t like that analogy at all and continued that “those things aren’t easily accessible for all of US”. This is where I could see a clear difference in the way we thought. I’m a Christian and I believe we should all be kind to each other. We are all God’s children and the Bible states we all came from one blood. I believe this through and through so skin color really doesn’t matter. I can develop strong relationships with people of all races and I never look for differences as a benchmark of how close I can get to another person. I believe this is a huge weight on the shoulders of many blacks. Too many of us grew up in a struggle and start to blame others. Instead of creating paths for ourselves, we argue that the “White Man” has cut off all paths to success and made them exclusive to their own race. Even if there is a such thing as White Privilege, it only affects those who are looking for an excuse for why they can’t achieve their own goals. In addition to being a Christian, I’m also ex Air Force. I’ve been able to join the military, attend college and get a good paying job…all while black. I don’t think that is by design according to my colleague, apparently I’m lucky. ...
